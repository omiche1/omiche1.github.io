Cos-positive

Hey guys! So this viedo is a little guide on being more comfortable with yourself when you cosplay. I focus a lot on the physical aspects of comfort becasue I firmly believe that you will feel better about yourself if you're in comfortable clothes. I also think that physical comfort changes how you act, and if you're in a better mood you'll have more funa nd foster a more positive community. I'm aware this isn't always a possibility, but I give some tips on how to do it. I also talk about some things to do to help your emotinoal comfort, because that's my main goal. I want to battle body shaming towards one's self as well as towaards other. This is by no means the ony way to go about cosplay, and if you have better ideas or stories or anything at all you'd like to comment on, please do so!

Story Time!

Here's a podcast for you to listen to! I interviewed 3 people with different experiences than mine so that you can get a larger view of the cosplay community. Dan, a 24 year old Filipino guy is more interested in prop making and being exact than anything. While he still wants to be comfortable he recognizes that it's difficult to achieve. Leila is an 8 year old girl who is trying to cosplay more, but right now only does Halloween. She brings up intersting points about preferring comfot to exactness and how she may laugh at a silly costume, but never wants to make anyone feel badly. Stefan is a 30 year old white man who has been in the convention scene for a long time, he talks about respecting people's boundaries and the unfai discrimination of revealing cosplays.

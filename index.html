What Now?

What the heck am I doing and why

If you've noticed, over the past few years there have been a lot of outcries about body shaming. Many people, especially women, are comitted to ending the false perceptions of people thiking they have to be unhealty to be beautiful. We've seen companies like Aerie use real people instead of models, Dove started a whole movement aimed toward young girls to love themselves, even actresses like Jenifer Lawrence are speaking out about how everyone's body is different.

I want to take this a step further; I want to adress body shaming in a community where people aren't even awar it's happening: Cosplay. As the cosplay community grows more cultural standards are placed on a formerly lax environment. Where nerds and geeks and fan used to be able to gather and simply enjoy themsleves and their favorite things, we now have preasure to be perfect. Much of the animostisy in the cosplay community comes from individuals who direct it at themselves, basically being overly critical. But we also have people who are unkind and presumtuous, who judge out loud and make people feel uncomfortable. Luckily, it hasn't yet permeated our community, so I'm just trying to stop it before it explodes.
